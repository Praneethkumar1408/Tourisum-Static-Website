Taj Mahal

The Golden Temple, or Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the preeminent spiritual shrine of Sikhism located in Amritsar, Punjab. Founded in the late 16th century by Guru Arjan Dev Ji, the temple was uniquely designed with four entrances to welcome people of all faiths and backgrounds, symbolizing universal brotherhood. Its stunning architecture features a central marble structure topped with 24-carat gold leaf, which glows brilliantly against the surrounding Amrit Sarovar (Pool of Nectar). Beyond its visual beauty, the temple is world-renowned for the Guru Ram Das Langar, a community kitchen that provides free meals to over 100,000 visitors daily, regardless of their status. As a living monument of peace and selfless service, it remains one of the most visited and revered sites on the planet.
