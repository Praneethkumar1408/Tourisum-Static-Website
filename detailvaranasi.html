Varanasi Temple

Varanasi, the spiritual heart of India, is one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, standing as a living monument to Hindu tradition and cultural evolution for over 3,000 years. Nestled along the sacred crescent-shaped bank of the Ganges, the city—also known as Kashi or Benares—is revered as the abode of Lord Shiva and a gateway to moksha, or liberation from the cycle of rebirth. Its landscape is defined by more than 80 iconic ghats, where daily life and death intersect through morning ritual baths and perpetual cremation ceremonies at sites like Manikarnika Ghat. Beyond its spiritual depth, Varanasi is a preeminent center for Indian classical music, Sanskrit learning, and the world-renowned Banarasi silk weaving industry. In February 2026, the city's vibrant cultural scene remains active with events like the Kashi Utsav and the Banaras Literature Festival, even as it modernizes with major infrastructure projects like India's first public urban ropeway system.
