Mysore Palace

The Mysuru Palace (Amba Vilas) is a masterpiece of Indo-Saracenic architecture, blending Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic styles Mysore Palace Official. Designed by British architect Henry Irwin and completed in 1912, it served as the royal seat of the Wadiyar dynasty Karnataka Tourism.

  1. Illminaton :On Sundays and public holidays, the palace is lit by nearly 97,000 bulbs, creating a breathtaking golden glow Mysore Palace Official.
  2. Golden Throne: The palace houses a magnificent jewelled throne made of 280kg of gold, displayed only during the Dasara Festival.
  3. Artistic Interiors: Highlights include the Kalyana Mantapa (marriage hall) with its peacock-themed stained glass and the grand Durbar Hall with its painted ceilings Google Arts & Culture.
  4. Public Access: It is the second most visited monument in India after the Taj Mahal, featuring a Sound and Light show that narrates its 600-year history.
